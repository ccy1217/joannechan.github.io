Food Waste Reduction

Overview

According to Safdie (2024), the problem of food waste has become a widespread issue, with over 30% of food being lost or wasted annually. We want to find ways to improve trust in food management systems among consumers, as the current status quo leads to uncertainty about food freshness and contributes to unnecessary waste. We believe that households and sustainability-conscious consumers will be interested in our solution if we can effectively address this issue. We will build a smart fridge system that uses AI to scan and track food items, notifying users when products are nearing expiration and suggesting ways to use them, cultivating a more efficient and sustainable approach to food consumption.

Target user group

  • Sustainability-conscious consumers
  • Busy households
  • Individuals interested in reducing food waste and saving on grocery expenses

    Histogram

    Output from the Session:

  • Our goal was to explore whether an AI fridge that tracks food expiration dates and suggests meals would be more useful than a traditional fridge in reducing food waste.
  • The survey compared users who manually track food expiration with those who don’t track expiration dates at all.
  • The hypothesis test showed a p-value of 0.0391, which is below 0.05. This indicates we reject the null hypothesis and accept the alternative hypothesis. There is a significant difference in how the two groups perceive the AI fridge’s usefulness.
  • People who don’t track food expiration dates rated the meal suggestion feature significantly higher than those who manually check. This suggests they struggle more with food management and would benefit from an AI fridge.
  • Users who already manually check expiration dates still saw some benefits in the AI fridge but did not find it as necessary.

    • New ideas

    Based on these findings, we could consider additional AI fridge features:

  • Smarter Expiration Tracking & Notifications. The fridge will use red-light indicators and mobile push notifications to remind users of expiring or expired food items.
  • Enhanced Barcode & Smart Tag Scanning. Users can scan barcodes via the fridge screen, manually input item details, or use smart tags for tracking non-barcoded items.
  • Reminder Function for Reducing Food Waste. By consistently notifying users about soon-to-expire food, the system helps prevent waste by encouraging timely consumption.
  • Cost-Efficient AI Camera Integration. A single AI camera in an acrylic enclosure will lower production costs while ensuring accurate inventory tracking.
  • Energy-Efficient AI Operation. The camera will only activate on schedule or user demand, reducing unnecessary energy consumption.
  • Expanding Market Reach Beyond High-Income Households . A more affordable AI fridge model will make the technology accessible to budget-conscious consumers.

    Final Design

  • The blueprint sketch of the Smart Fridge includes a freezer part and a cooler part. It features a smart monitor, label printer, ice maker, camera, and scanners.
  • It shows a more clear blueprint sketch for reference.The camera and scanner at the front of the fridge detect and scan food labels or barcodes. If no label is provided, users can use the smart monitor to print an edible label or utilize the provided NFC tags to label the food and register it in the inventory system.
  • The internal design features a camera and scanner on top of the fridge for detecting and scanning food labels. The movable AI camera detects rotten food in different food cabinets, and the storage plate lights up when rotten food is detected in the respective cabinet.
  • The cameras and scanners featured on top of the fridge, it scan food labels using embedded image scanning software. Additionally, the scanners scan the NFC tags on all the cabinets below. If any stored food item's expiration date has passed, the system will send an alert to notify the user.
  • In addition to scanning food labels, the cameras of the fridge perform image detection. The image detection system uses advanced AI image recognition to identify rotten food and alert the user if spoiled items are detected.
  • It shows the movable AI camera that can move up and down to scan food in different cabinets. This allows the camera to detect spoiled or rotten food across various sections of the fridge, ensuring thorough monitoring.
  • The food plate will light up if the system detects spoiled or expired food, providing a visual alert to the user about the condition of the items in the respective cabinet.

    • Extra Features:

  • Smart Thermometer: Automatically adjust the temperature and humidity to keep food fresh longer
  • AI Energy Save Mode: It reduces the energy usage with AI
  • Smart Tag/ Smart Clip: For food that does not come with a food label or bar code.
